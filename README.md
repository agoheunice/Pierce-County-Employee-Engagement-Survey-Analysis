## Introduction:

This project analyzes employee survey responses to evaluate employee engagement, workplace satisfaction, and organizational performance across departments and roles. 

The dataset was cleaned and transformed using Microsoft Excel Power Query, analyzed with PivotTables, and presented a dashboard featuring KPIs, and charts. 
The analysis aims to identify engagement strengths, highlight areas requiring improvement, and provide evidence-based recommendations to support better workplace decision-making.

### Analysis:

The survey data was cleaned by correcting data types, removing inconsistencies, and creating a new Role column from multiple binary role indicators to simplify analysis. During the cleaning process, a significant data quality issue was identified, with approximately 72% of respondents lacking a specified role, which was documented as a limitation for role-based analysis.

The analysis examined employee responses across survey questions, departments, and roles to identify engagement patterns. Results showed that employees generally understood their job expectations and responsibilities, while questions relating to workplace relationships and employee engagement received comparatively lower levels of agreement. Department level comparisons highlighted variations in employee sentiment, and role based analysis provided additional insight into differences in engagement among identified employee groups.

A dashboard was developed to enable users and stakeholders to explore employee engagement trends across different organizational segments.


## Conclusion and Recommendations:

The analysis indicates that while employees generally have a clear understanding of their roles and responsibilities, opportunities exist to strengthen employee engagement, collaboration, and workplace relationships. Organizations should prioritize initiatives that improve recognition, communication, and team culture while continuing to monitor engagement across departments.

The project also highlights the importance of improving data collection practices by ensuring employee roles are consistently captured during future surveys. Addressing this data quality issue will enable more reliable role-based analysis and support better workforce decision-making.
